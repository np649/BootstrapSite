History of Formula One

Formula One can trace it's roots all the way back to the European Grand Prix's which were help in the early 1920's and 30's. The sport itself was formally organized in 1946 by the FIA but did not have many races until Europe was rebuilt after World War Two.

The First Grand Prix

The first race to be held under the name Grand Prix was held in Le Mans, France in 1906. The race was almost 65 miles per lap and only had three turns. Thus Maurice Farman was awarded the 'Grand Prix de Pau' for his overall victory in the Circuit du Sud-Ouest driving a Panhard 24 hp.
